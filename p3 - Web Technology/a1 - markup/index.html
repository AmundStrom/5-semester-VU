More about the artist

Every Kanye album portrays a certain period his life. Thus to understand what a Kanye album is all about, we must look at where Kanye as a person was at the time of making. Table below explains the meaning behind his first four ablbums:

First four Kanye ablbums
Poster Name Year Genre Description
The Collage Dropout February 2004
  • Chipmunk Soul
  • Pop Rap
 This album is about Kanye being an outsider in the music industry. He was in it but for other people, later ultimately for himself. As a college dropout himself, West uses the cover art and other skits as a criticism on the educational system and peoples expectations of school, while he claims connection pawave the educational way .
Late Registration
August 2005

You found me!
  • Hip Hop
  • Pop Rap
 This album focuses on Kanye's transition into celebrity: After the massive popularity of TCD, Kanye had a taste of fame but still wasn't used to fame. It was a huge adjustment from where he had been prior. This album is about Kanye starting to understand what fame offers and what it could cost him. The album also promots his signature production style known as chipmunk soul, which is using sped-up vocal samples from soul records.
Graduation
September 2007

You Found me!
  • Pop Rap
 The message of this album is that he "graduated" to success. Fully transitioned into fame, which allows him to creat whatever he wants and not care how other people react. In comparison to previous albums, which were largely driven by observational commentary on matters dealing with social standers, Graduation is more introspective and addresses personal themes.
808s & Heartbreak September 2008
  • Electropop
  • Synthpop
  • Art Pop
 This 2008 release was his first after the passing of his mother, Donda. She played a large role in his life being his only primary care giver. This album was Kanye's way of expreasing his inner termoil, created not only by his mother's passing but also the dissolution of his 18-month engagement to Alexis Phifer.
